George Brown College is a public college located in downtown Toronto, Ontario.
Founded in 1967, it was named after George Brown, a prominent Canadian journalist
and one of the Fathers of Confederation. The college has grown to serve over
30,000 full-time students and offers more than 160 programs across various disciplines.

George Brown College is known for its strong emphasis on experiential learning.
Students gain real-world experience through co-op placements, internships,
and field education with some of Toronto's top employers and organizations.

The college operates three main campuses in Toronto:
- St. James Campus: Business, Hospitality, and Arts programs
- Casa Loma Campus: Construction, Engineering, and Technology
- Waterfront Campus: Health Sciences and Community Services
Each campus is conveniently located near public transit in the heart of the city.

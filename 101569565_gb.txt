George Brown College is a public college located in Toronto, Ontario, Canada.
It was founded in 1967 and is named after George Brown, a Father of Confederation.
The college serves over 30,000 full-time students across multiple campuses.

George Brown offers over 160 full-time programs spanning multiple industries.
The college is particularly well known for its programs in technology, business,
health sciences, hospitality, and the arts. Many programs include mandatory
co-op terms where students gain paid work experience before graduation.

George Brown has three main campuses:
- St. James Campus (School of Business)
- Casa Loma Campus (School of Technology)
- Waterfront Campus (School of Health Sciences)

The downtown Toronto location gives students direct access to industry employers and networking.

Programs range from certificates and diplomas to bachelor degrees.
